Q: What is cabbage?
A: Cabbage is a leafy green vegetable in the Brassica family. It forms a tight, round head of layered leaves.

Q: How do you grow cabbage?
A: Cabbage grows best in cool weather with full sun, moist soil, and consistent watering. It prefers temperatures between 10°C and 20°C.

Q: What are the main types of cabbage?
A: Green cabbage, red cabbage, Savoy cabbage, Napa cabbage, and pointed cabbage.

Q: How long does cabbage take to grow?
A: Most cabbage varieties take 70 to 120 days from seed to harvest.

Q: What nutrients are in cabbage?
A: Cabbage is rich in vitamin C, vitamin K, fiber, folate, and antioxidants.

Q: How big can a cabbage get?
A: A typical cabbage weighs 1 to 3 pounds, but giant varieties can exceed 50 pounds.

Q: Did you know?
A: The world record for the heaviest cabbage is over 138 pounds.

Q: Where is cabbage grown?
A: Cabbage is grown worldwide, with major production in China, India, Russia, South Korea, and the United States.

Q: How many cabbages are grown each year?
A: Millions of tons of cabbage are harvested annually across the globe.

Q: What can you cook with cabbage?
A: Cabbage can be used in soups, salads, stir-fries, rolls, kimchi, sauerkraut, and roasted dishes.

Q: Why is cabbage good for you?
A: It supports digestion, boosts immunity, and provides anti-inflammatory compounds.

Q: Fun fact:
A: Red cabbage can be used as a natural pH indicator because its pigments change color in acidic or basic solutions.

Q: Another fun fact:
A: Cabbage has been cultivated for over 4,000 years and was prized by ancient civilizations.

Q: How do you store cabbage?
A: Keep whole heads in the refrigerator. They can last 2 to 3 weeks if uncut.

Q: What pests affect cabbage?
A: Cabbage worms, aphids, slugs, and flea beetles are common pests.

Q: How do you protect cabbage plants?
A: Use row covers, companion planting, and regular inspection to prevent pest damage.

Q: What is sauerkraut?
A: Sauerkraut is fermented cabbage made by combining shredded cabbage with salt and allowing it to ferment naturally.

Q: What is kimchi?
A: Kimchi is a Korean fermented dish made with cabbage, radish, garlic, chili, and spices.

Q: Why does cabbage smell when cooked?
A: Overcooking releases sulfur compounds that create a strong odor.

Q: What is the average size of a full-grown cabbage?
A: Most cabbages reach 6 to 10 inches in diameter depending on the variety.

Q: How much water does cabbage need?
A: Cabbage requires consistent moisture, about 1 to 1.5 inches of water per week.

Q: Can cabbage survive frost?
A: Yes. Cabbage is frost-tolerant and can survive light freezes.

Q: What is the best soil for cabbage?
A: Well-drained, fertile soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5.

Q: Why do people love cabbage?
A: It’s versatile, nutritious, affordable, and grows well in many climates.
